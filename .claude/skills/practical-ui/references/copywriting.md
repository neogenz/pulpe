# Copywriting & Microcopy

## Be Concise

- Remove every word that adds no information.
- Avoid filler: "actually", "basically", "really", "quite".
- Drop articles where possible: "a", "an", "the".
- Kill introductory phrases: "would you like to", "in order to", "are you sure".
- Use shorter words over longer synonyms.
- **Sentences under 20 words.**

## Sentence Case

- Use sentence case everywhere (capitalise only first word + proper nouns).
- Title case is harder to read — capitals interrupt scanning.

## Plain Language

- Write as if speaking to a 6th grader unfamiliar with the topic.
- No jargon, no slang.
- Short, simple words > long, complex ones.
- Use contractions: who's, they're, you're.

## Front-Load Text

- Put the most important info at the **start** of headings, lists, and links.
- Users scan down the left edge — key info must come first.
- "5 UI design eBooks you should read" > "You should read these 5 UI design eBooks".

## Inverted Pyramid

1. **Heading**: most important information (action or conclusion).
2. **Body**: supporting details for those who need more.
3. **Background**: defer to a separate screen via "Learn more".

## Numbers

- Use **numerals** (899), not words (eight hundred and ninety nine).
- Format large numbers with commas: 1,000 not 1000.
- Very large: combine numeral + word: "1 billion".

## Abbreviations & Acronyms

- Avoid them. Spell out the full term.
- "Apartment number" > "Apt. no."

## Break Up Content

- Large text blocks → smaller pieces with **descriptive headings** and bullet points.
- Headings must make sense **out of context** (screen readers navigate by heading list).
- "Beautiful waterfront location" > "Location".

## Form Labels

- Don't use "My" on labels (interface speaks to user, not as user).
- "Your" is clearer but usually unnecessary.
- Prefer the simplest label: "Email" > "My email" > "Your email".

## Vocabulary Consistency

- Same word for the same concept throughout the product.
- Don't mix: publish/post, sign up/register, log in/sign in, delete/remove, subscribe/join.

## Full Stops

- No periods on short UI text unless it's a full sentence with commas.
- Be consistent: all periods or no periods across similar elements.

## Matching Text Lengths

- When text blocks sit in a row (feature cards), write them to roughly the **same length**.
- Matching lengths = aligned layout = cleaner look.

## Descriptive Links

- Never use "learn more", "read more", "click here".
- Link text must describe the destination: "Explore templates", "Email marketing features".
- Consider turning headings into links instead of adding separate link text.

## Error Messages

- Communicate: **what happened**, **why**, and **how to fix it**.
- Never blame the user.
- Skip "please", "sorry", "oops".
- Make headings and buttons descriptive so users don't need to read body text.
- "Payment failed — Update your payment details and try again" > "Oops, something went wrong!"
